What To Do Before Class

1
Choose Your Research Page

Look through your portfolio and select a research page that:

  • Has room for additional content
  • Would benefit from more depth (quotes, references, secondary images)
  • Connects to a topic you want to explore further

Write down the page title/topic.

2
Choose Your Digital Skill

Review the Quick Reference list above and decide which technique you want to use:

  • Consider: Which skill did you find most interesting?
  • Consider: Which skill would enhance your chosen research page?
  • Consider: Do you want to improve at a skill you found challenging?

Write down your chosen skill and review the tutorial link.

3
Gather Your Materials

Based on your chosen skill, collect the relevant materials:

  • Physical samples (monoprints, photocopies, textures) to scan
  • Digital files you've already created
  • Reference images that inspire your direction

Bring these to class or have them saved digitally.

4
Prepare Your A3 Page

Make sure your chosen research page is ready to scan:

  • Any wet media should be completely dry
  • Loose elements should be glued down
  • The page should be flat (not curled or bent)
5
Optional: Start Finding Your Secondary Image

If you have time, begin researching secondary images related to your research page topic:

  • Look for artist works, photographs, or diagrams that expand your research
  • Use credible sources (museum websites, artist portfolios, academic sources)
  • Note down the source information for Harvard referencing

This is optional — you will have time in class, but starting early helps!

⚠️

Don't Skip the Preparation!

If you arrive to class without deciding which page and which skill to focus on, you will lose valuable working time.

  • Make your decisions before the lesson
  • Review the tutorial for your chosen skill
  • Gather materials relevant to your technique

💭 Questions to Consider

  • Which research page in my portfolio feels incomplete or could use more depth?
  • Which digital skill did I find most interesting or want to improve at?
  • How can the digital technique enhance rather than overwhelm my physical work?
  • What materials do I already have that could work with my chosen technique?
💡

Helpful Tips

  • Review the tutorial: Click on your chosen skill above and skim through the steps so you're familiar with the process
  • Think about integration: Your digital additions should complement your physical work, not cover it up
  • Prepare scanning: Know where the scanner is or have a scanning app ready on your phone (Adobe Scan or Microsoft Lens)
  • Save reference images: If you find inspiration, save it to your phone or a folder
